Sebastian Vettel

"The Finger"

Here's a timeline of his F1 career:

2007:

  • Vettel made his Formula 1 debut at the 2007 United States Grand Prix for BMW Sauber as a stand-in for Robert Kubica.
  • He became the youngest point-scorer in F1 at the time when he finished in 8th place.

2008:

  • Vettel signed with Scuderia Toro Rosso, Red Bull's sister team, for the full season.
  • He secured his first pole position and race win at the 2008 Italian Grand Prix.
  • Vettel finished the season in 8th place in the Drivers' Championship.

2009:

  • Vettel continued with Toro Rosso until the 2009 British Grand Prix when he moved to the Red Bull Racing team to replace David Coulthard.
  • He won his first race with Red Bull Racing at the 2009 Chinese Grand Prix.
  • Finished the season as the runner-up in the Drivers' Championship, narrowly losing out to Jenson Button.

2010:

  • Vettel had a dominant season, winning his first World Drivers' Championship.
  • He became the youngest World Champion in F1 history at that time.
  • Vettel secured 10 race wins during the season.

2011:

  • Vettel had a dominant season, winning his first World Drivers' Championship.
  • He achieved 11 race victories during the season.

2012:

  • Vettel secured his third consecutive World Championship title.
  • He won 5 races during the season.

2013:

  • Vettel continued his dominance, securing his fourth consecutive World Championship title.
  • He won 13 races during the season, tying Michael Schumacher's record for most wins in a single season.

2014:

  • Vettel faced a challenging season with technical issues and finished 5th in the Drivers' Championship.
  • It was announced that he would be leaving Red Bull Racing at the end of the season.

2015:

  • Vettel joined Scuderia Ferrari for the 2015 season.
  • He won three races during his first season with Ferrari and finished 3rd in the Drivers' Championship.

2016-2020:

  • Vettel continued to drive for Ferrari but was unable to secure a World Championship title.
  • He consistently finished in the top 3 in the Drivers' Championship during these years.

2021:

  • Vettel signed with the Aston Martin (formerly Racing Point) team for the 2021 season.
  • This marked a new chapter in his F1 career as he joined a different team for the first time in many years.
